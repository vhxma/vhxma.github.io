Starting Out

Today marks the end of my first three weeks at Microsoft and if I had to illustrate what that meant for me, it might go something like this:

From left-right and clockwise: Onboarding Training, Coffee Catch-ups, Teams Call, a CHRISTMAS party(!!), Helping with an Outreach Presentation, Lots of Information Sometimes

At first I was confused. Very confused. I came in raring to go and instead, the only expectation my manager had of me was to meet people and do training. It was weird being paid to drink coffee and sit at my computer to learn. But let me tell you, there was a LOT of information so I’m glad I had the time to sit down, and take it all in. I realised that it would be a while before I would know all the technicalities involved with being a CSA. Instead of dwelling on the fact that it felt a bit unstructured, I decided that I would take the opportunity to take it easy, meet people and pick their brains. When else would I get the chance to? And it’s turned out to be a lot of fun. So while at the beginning I would say I was:

  • Confused
  • Wondering about what I could do
  • Definitely wondering about what I was supposed to do

Now, I have a better idea about what I want to accomplish here. I want to make the most of my time here at Microsoft and learn about different areas of the tech industry that I might not have considered before. I would like to find a way to make social impact. I also want to continue talking to those around me and get to know my colleagues. But above all, I would like to enjoy my experience and by the end, feel like I have improved upon myself.
